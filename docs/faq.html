Bio-Reactive Features

Echoelmusic reads your heart rate and heart rate variability (HRV) in real-time via HealthKit (iOS) or Health Connect (Android). These biometrics are used to:

  • Adjust tempo: Music speed adapts to your heart rate
  • Modify harmony: Chord progressions respond to HRV coherence
  • Control dynamics: Volume and intensity reflect your state
  • Drive visualizations: Particle systems pulse with your heartbeat

All processing happens on-device, ensuring your biometric data stays private.

Heart Rate Variability (HRV) coherence is a measure of the regularity of your heart rhythm patterns. High coherence is associated with:

  • Reduced stress
  • Improved focus
  • Emotional balance
  • Flow states

Echoelmusic uses coherence to adjust the music, creating a feedback loop that may help you achieve and maintain coherent states.

Wellness & Science

Binaural beats are an auditory phenomenon where two slightly different frequencies (one per ear) create the perception of a third "beat" frequency.

Example: 432 Hz in left ear + 442 Hz in right ear = 10 Hz perceived beat (alpha waves)

Different beat frequencies correspond to brainwave states:

  • Delta (0.5-4 Hz): Deep sleep, healing
  • Theta (4-8 Hz): Meditation, creativity
  • Alpha (8-14 Hz): Relaxation, learning
  • Beta (14-30 Hz): Focus, alertness
  • Gamma (30+ Hz): Peak awareness, cognition

Note: Binaural beats require headphones. Echoelmusic automatically switches to isochronic tones when using speakers.

The science behind Echoelmusic draws from multiple research areas:

  • HRV Biofeedback: Extensively studied for stress reduction (Lehrer & Gevirtz, 2014)
  • Music Therapy: Recognized by the American Music Therapy Association
  • Entrainment: Auditory-cortical synchronization research (Thaut, 2005)
  • Coherence: HeartMath Institute research on HRV patterns

Disclaimer: Echoelmusic is a wellness tool, not a medical device. It is not intended to diagnose, treat, or cure any condition.

Echoelmusic offers 432 Hz as an optional tuning reference (vs. standard 440 Hz). Some users report 432 Hz feels more "natural" or calming.

Troubleshooting

Try these steps:

  1. Ensure HealthKit/Health Connect access is granted in Settings
  2. Check that your Apple Watch/heart rate monitor is connected
  3. Start a workout on your watch to activate continuous heart rate monitoring
  4. Restart the Echoelmusic app
  5. On iOS: Settings > Privacy > Health > Echoelmusic > Enable Heart Rate

Audio glitches usually indicate CPU overload. Try:

  • Increasing buffer size in Settings > Audio > Buffer Size
  • Reducing particle count in Visualization settings
  • Closing other apps
  • On older devices: Enable Legacy Mode in Settings > Performance
